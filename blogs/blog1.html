I'm done
Volunteering Trip in Italy


During the summer of 2023, after completing the second year of my bachelor's degree in the US, I decided to travel back home to the Basque Country to visit family and friends and take a break from studying. As we end school earlier than in European countries, I decided to travel somewhere during June while my friends finished their exams.

As I wanted to save money for college, I didn’t want to spend a lot of my money on a trip. For this reason, I started looking for volunteering opportunities. I came across a program called Woofing where you can be hosted at an organic farm in exchange for volunteering. I thought this was a good idea so next I had to choose a country. My first idea was Italy, as it’s a neighboring country and I had never been there. Specifically, I was interested in a beautiful peninsula south of Naples, which I had seen on social media. I soon learned that it was called Amalfi. With no hesitation, I made an account in Woof and started looking for farms in the area. Of the three options I found, two were already full, but luckily I was accepted in the third place. We discussed options and finally, I flew there on May 28.

Although Italy is near, I spent a long time traveling there. I took a flight from Bilbao to Barcelona, and then another one to Naples (I couldn’t find direct flights). Then I had to take a long bus ride from the airport to Piano di Sorrento. I really enjoyed the views and got a taste of what I’d be seeing. I was surprised by all the Football decorations they had everywhere, in every single building and street. Naples had recently won the Italian league so this might be why. Once I arrived, I walked to the farm and met my host family and their son Tommasso. There was also another girl volunteering there from Belgium. She had lived the entire year going from farm to farm! I communicated with them in English. I could understand some Italian because it’s quite similar to Spanish. I learned about their goals. They wanted to become self-sufficient and produce everything they consumed.

I quickly started learning what kind of work I’d be doing and the daily routines. Every day I woke up at around 6:50 and quickly got ready to work at 7. First I took measurements like temperature and precipitation. Following that, I removed weeds from the small garden (especially a plant called Convolvolo). Next, I fed the chicken, dogs, cats, and pigs. After that, it was up to my hosts to come up with the daily project. It could be anything from cutting wood with an ax to building poles to help tomatoes grow. I also did some chores like cleaning, cooking, and washing dishes. Around 11 or 12, I was done for the day and we had lunch. Then I was free to explore the area until dinner. I walked a lot during this time, the peninsula was much larger than I expected and I struggled to understand the complex public transportation system. I would study an area on Google Earth and then I would walk there. Many times I improvised and took random trails I found, and usually, I found great views. In addition, I got two free days per week and they were flexible about it. I will share a few highlights of the trip.

On my first free day, I went on a big hike. I hiked up 12km nonstop from a road until arriving at the town of Santa Maria Del Castello. From here I had my first amazing view of one of the most famous towns in the world, Positano. Positano is a cliffside village that is a well-known holiday destination with a pebble beachfront and steep, narrow streets lined with boutiques and cafes. The green mountains contrasted with the blue ocean and colorful buildings. I then started the descent of 900m loss in only one kilometer. To my surprise, I was the only one doing it. I would then learn that the entire place is full of rich tourists that don’t explore outside the popular spots. Positano became one of the most beautiful places I have been to. After walking around the town, I decided to take the bus back as I was tired and knew that I would return later on in the trip.

On the first Friday of June, my host family recommended going to Pomei as it was free that day. I thought it was a good idea so I took the train there. The train went through many tunnels, it was full of tourists, and it was very noisy. In addition, I was told I had to pay the ticket directly to the conductor, which I still don’t understand. Once I arrived, I waited in line along with thousands of other tourists and finally entered the well-known ruins. I was lucky and had views of the Vesuvius volcano, which caused the destruction of this ancient town. I spend about 3 hours roaming around old buildings and exploring every corner. I also encountered a few cats sleeping.

After a while, the weather became very hot so I decided to stop in a town I crossed on the train. Castellammare was a nice town with a lot of people hanging out. I then found a cable car that went up Monte Faito. It was a bit cloudy at the top but I still got a ticket. At the top, there were a few restaurants. I got ice cream and went to the peak. The views I encountered were fantastic and I spent a long time taking pictures and just relaxing with the views.

Another memorable experience was going to Amalfi. Along with Positano, Amalfi was a very famous destination. I woke up early, around 6 am, and got on the bus. The main reason was that later buses leaving from Sorrento usually filled up at the first station. After over an hour of trip through curvy roads on steep cliffs, I arrived quite early. I visited the cathedral of Saint Andrea first, it was very unique. Then, I started hiking up the Mulini Valley but got lost. So then I turned around and started walking towards Atrini, another town right next by with a very good reputation. It was also very beautiful. Then, I started hiking up all the way to Ravello. Most people took a taxi but I didn’t want to spend over 100$ on a short trip. Also, I could stop to see the cool views. I arrived at the top and explored this incredible town. The main attractions there were villas, Villa Rufolo and Villa Cimbrone. Both were beautiful mansions with botanic gardens. They had gorgeous views of the entire Amalfi Coast. Then I climbed a bit more to Pontone, where I wanted to check out a spot I found online. To my surprise again, I was alone. Tourists must not like to walk. I had 360 views from above the three towns I had just visited. In addition I could see two huge ships on the coast. Finally, I looped back to Amalfi where I had a swim and tasted some expensive lemon sorbet out of a lemon. I would have liked to stay longer, but I was tired and took the bus back home.

On my next free day, I wanted to go on a boat, so I booked a round trip to Capri. At the time I didn’t know how prestigious this island was. It is worldwide known for being a destination for millionaires and famous people due to its beautiful landscape, coast, expensive restaurants, and designer fashion stores like LV or Gucci. I wasn’t planning on spending all my money on clothes, so I went on another hike instead. I landed in Marina Grande and took some steep stairs that took me straight to Anacapri. Then my goal was to submit to Monte Solare, the highest peak on the island. Most people took the chairlift from Anacapri, but I was feeling good so I quickly got to the top and took photos on the way. At first, I couldn’t see anything due to the clouds. But after ten minutes the clouds started to disappear and I could see the entire island from above. There was a cool cliff that looked like Hawaii.

Then I hiked down to a popular beach in Marina Piccola. I had a refreshing swim until I got stung by a jellyfish. It burnt at first but after a while, I was fine thanks to the lifeguard's treatment. I then visited the main town of Capri. Everything was very expensive so I bought some fruit for lunch. Maybe one day if I make a lot of money I can return to explore that aspect of the island. In the end, I took the boat back to Sorrento and walked back home late at night.

On my last free day, it was raining a lot so I decided to explore Sorrento. This town was the main city where most people stayed. It was quite beautiful and had many stores with lemon products. The next day was supposed to be nice, so after work I borrowed my host family’s electric bike and I traveled 16 km to Nocelle. This was the end of the well-known Path of the Gods trail, which I did in reverse and only until the half point. The views were incredible as you can see in the photos. Then I biked down to Positano, where I explored some more and went for a swim (didn’t get stung this time). Like this day, I also visited other places during work days. Some of the most memorable can include Bagni Regina Giovanna, Fiordo di Crapolla, Meta, and Fiordo di Furore.

Finally, after 20 days there, I was done with my volunteer work and it was time to leave. I’m grateful I had the opportunity to stay at that farm. I learned a lot from the family and enjoyed spending time with the other volunteer and Tommasso. The next three days, I would slowly make my way back home while visiting three major cities.

First I stopped in Naples. Unlike other cities in Europe, Naples was very dirty, noisy, and chaotic. However, I liked that because it made it unique. Every single street was full of Naples football-themed decorations. I could see many images of Maradona as well, who for some was the greatest player of all time. I visited the main tourist attractions such as the San Marino Castle, the Toledo metro station, Galleria Umberto, and Piazza del Plebiscito. I also tried traditional Neapolitan Pizza. At the end of the day, I had my first experience staying at a hostel. It wasn’t very modern but it was cool to stay with other young people traveling like me.

The next morning I took a train to Rome. There was so much I wanted to see there that I spent 12 hours straight roaming the city. I visited iconic places like the Vatican, Saint Angelo Castle, Pantheon, Fontana di Trevi, Roman Forum, Circus Maximus, and the iconic Coliseum. Of course, I didn’t have time to enter all of them but they were nice from outside anyways. The night atmosphere was really nice. I returned to my new hostel (this one was nicer) and went to sleep to catch my flight the next morning to Barcelona.

I had been near Barcelona a few times before, but never in the city center. For this reason, I decided to spend a night there and explore the city. I was surprised with how nice it was, it surpassed my expectations. There was a lively atmosphere and unlike in the previous two cities, tourists blended in well with local people making it a unique experience. I liked how organized the city was. During my time there I visited the Sagrada Familia, Guinardo Park, Ciutadella Park, Casa Mila, and the National Art Museum of Catalunya. I returned to my third hostel (the best out of the three) and took the train back home the next day.

I will never forget this great experience. Not only did I learn about Italian culture, but I got out of my comfort zone by traveling alone to an unknown place and making my own decisions. I also learned from my mistakes and I bet I will be more prepared for my next adventure. If you made it this far, thanks for reading and I recommend doing a similar trip.
